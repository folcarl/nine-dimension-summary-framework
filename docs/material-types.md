# Material Types

## Short Text

Use lightweight mode when the material is brief, low-density, and low-stakes. Still include a real limitation and scores.

## Interviews And Oral History

Use full mode. Focus on memory reliability, editing choices, narrative gaps, and facts requiring verification.

## Lectures

Use full mode. Focus on concepts, argument path, evidence network, and whether the conclusion is overextended.

## Commentary And Analysis

Use full mode unless clearly short. Focus on emotional framing, certainty claims, alternative explanations, and audience risk.

## Economic, Investment, And Research Material

Use full mode. Include variables, transmission mechanisms, assumptions, indicators, risks, and judgment boundaries. Do not give investment advice.

## Political And Historical Material

Use full mode. Include historical context, institutional structure, competing positions, and ethical costs.

## Artistic Or Documentary Material

Use full mode for complex work. Focus on form/content relation, aesthetic strategy, emotional effect, and narrative or ethical limits.
